# NAX30 Index Committee Charter  
Namibia 30 Index (NAX30)

Version 2.0 – Tier-1 Institutional Governance Framework  
Effective Date: February 2026  

---

## 1. Purpose

This Charter establishes the structure, authority, composition, and operating procedures of the NAX30 Index Committee (the “Committee”).

The Committee is responsible for overseeing the methodology, constituent selection, rebalancing decisions, and structural integrity of the Namibia 30 Index (NAX30).

The Committee operates within the governance framework defined by the NAX30 smart contract architecture, Multi-Signature Safe, and TimelockController.

Preservation of benchmark integrity supersedes commercial, token issuance, exchange, or trading considerations.

---

## 2. Mandate

The Committee’s mandate includes:

- Oversight of index methodology  
- Review and approval of quarterly rebalancing  
- Approval of constituent additions or removals  
- Oversight of corporate action adjustments  
- Monitoring of eligibility compliance  
- Review of extraordinary events impacting constituents  
- Ensuring alignment with published methodology  

The Committee does not engage in discretionary market timing or active portfolio management.

The Committee does not consider token market price performance when making benchmark decisions.

---

## 3. Authority

The Committee has authority to:

- Propose weight adjustments  
- Propose constituent changes  
- Approve corporate action handling  
- Recommend methodology updates  
- Initiate extraordinary reviews  

All structural changes must:

1. Receive Committee approval  
2. Be executed through Multi-Signature Safe authorization  
3. Be scheduled via TimelockController  
4. Be recorded on-chain  

The Committee cannot bypass governance controls.

Regulatory directives, where applicable, supersede Committee discretion.

---

## 4. Composition

### 4.1 Membership Structure

The Committee shall consist of:

- Minimum of three (3) members  
- Individuals with demonstrable experience in capital markets, financial regulation, index methodology, or quantitative finance  

Members may include representatives affiliated with the Benchmark Administrator, provided independence safeguards are maintained.

---

### 4.2 Qualification Criteria

Members should possess:

- Relevant financial market expertise  
- Understanding of benchmark governance principles  
- No unresolved material conflict of interest  
- Professional competence sufficient to evaluate index methodology  

Appointments require conflict screening prior to confirmation.

---

### 4.3 Appointment and Term

Members are appointed through internal administrative process.

Standard term length: two (2) years, renewable.

Members may be removed for cause, including:

- Conflict of interest breach  
- Misconduct  
- Incapacity  
- Regulatory disqualification  
- Failure to perform duties  

Replacement follows formal governance documentation.

---

### 4.4 Chairperson

The Committee shall designate a Chair.

The Chair is responsible for:

- Convening meetings  
- Establishing agenda  
- Presiding over deliberations  
- Ensuring documentation of decisions  

In case of tie vote, the matter is deferred for further review. The Chair does not exercise unilateral override authority.

---

## 5. Independence & Commercial Separation

The Committee operates independently from:

- Token issuance strategy  
- Exchange listing negotiations  
- Market-making operations  
- Revenue objectives  
- Token holders  
- Trading venues  

Commercial considerations must not influence:

- Constituent selection  
- Weighting decisions  
- Corporate action treatment  
- Methodology amendments  

Benchmark integrity remains the primary objective.

---

## 6. Meeting Frequency

The Committee shall meet:

- At minimum quarterly (aligned with rebalancing cycle)  
- Additionally as required in extraordinary circumstances  

Extraordinary meetings may be convened in cases of:

- Delisting  
- Regulatory action  
- Corporate restructuring  
- Market disruption  
- Integrity concerns  

---

## 7. Voting Procedures

- Each member has one vote.  
- Decisions require simple majority unless otherwise specified.  
- Quorum requires majority of appointed members.  

Emergency decisions require majority vote and must be formally documented.

All votes must be recorded in meeting minutes.

---

## 8. Rebalancing Oversight

### 8.1 Scheduled Review

Quarterly reviews include:

- Weight normalization  
- Concentration control enforcement  
- Sector representation assessment  
- Liquidity reassessment  

### 8.2 Extraordinary Events

Extraordinary review may occur in cases of:

- Delisting  
- Prolonged trading suspension  
- Corporate restructuring  
- Regulatory intervention  

All decisions require formal documentation and governance execution.

---

## 9. Eligibility Oversight

The Committee ensures that constituents:

- Remain listed on the Namibia Securities Exchange (NSX)  
- Maintain regulatory good standing  
- Exhibit continuous trading status  
- Meet minimum liquidity standards  

If a constituent fails eligibility criteria, the Committee may recommend replacement.

---

## 10. Methodology Amendments

Methodology updates require:

1. Committee review  
2. Formal approval vote  
3. Governance execution via Safe + Timelock  
4. Public documentation update  

Methodology changes cannot be retroactively applied.

---

## 11. Emergency Decision Framework

In urgent circumstances affecting benchmark integrity:

- Emergency meeting may be convened  
- Majority vote required  
- Governance scheduling remains mandatory  
- Timelock cannot be bypassed unless explicitly coded in governance architecture  

All emergency decisions must be documented and archived.

---

## 12. Conflict of Interest

Members must:

- Disclose material conflicts  
- Abstain from voting where conflict exists  
- Act in good faith  

Conflicts are documented.

---

## 13. Recordkeeping

The Committee maintains:

- Minutes of meetings  
- Records of decisions  
- Voting outcomes  
- Rationale for changes  

Records are archived for minimum five (5) years.

---

## 14. Transparency

Publicly available:

- Current methodology  
- Constituent list  
- Governance contract addresses  
- Executed structural changes  

Committee deliberations remain confidential unless structural change is implemented.

---

## 15. External Advisors

The Committee may consult independent advisors for:

- Corporate action treatment  
- Methodology evaluation  
- Regulatory interpretation  

Advisors have no voting authority.

---

## 16. Succession & Administrator Disruption

If the Benchmark Administrator ceases operations:

- Governance may designate successor administrative authority  
- Committee structure may be reconstituted via governance  
- All actions remain subject to smart contract control  

---

## 17. Regulatory Alignment

The Committee operates in accordance with:

- Applicable Namibian law  
- Published methodology  
- Governance smart contract controls  

Regulatory requirements supersede discretionary decisions.

---

## 18. Limitation of Liability

Committee members act in administrative capacity.

Members are not fiduciaries and do not provide investment advice.

The Committee does not guarantee:

- Market performance  
- Continuous index calculation  
- Market price stability  

---

## 19. Review of Charter

This Charter may be reviewed annually.

Amendments require:

1. Committee approval  
2. Governance execution  
3. Public documentation update  

---

End of Index Committee Charter.
