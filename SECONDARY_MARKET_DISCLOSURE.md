# NAX30 SECONDARY MARKET DISCLOSURE

Namibia 30 Index (NAX30)

By [Simon Kapenda](https://linkedin.com/in/simonkapenda)       
Version 1.0  
Effective Date: February 2026  

---

## 1. Purpose

Namibia 30 Index (NAX30) tokens may be admitted for trading on secondary market venues, including centralized exchanges (CEXs), decentralized exchanges (DEXs), Over-the-Counter (OTC), or other trading platforms, subject to independent venue approval and applicable regulatory considerations.

This Secondary Market Disclosure outlines the structural characteristics, risks, and limitations associated with potential secondary market trading of NAX30 tokens.

This document is intended to promote transparency, market integrity, and regulatory clarity.

---

## 2. No Guarantee of Liquidity

Secondary market liquidity is not guaranteed.

Trading activity on any centralized exchange (CEX), decentralized exchange (DEX), or over-the-counter (OTC) venue is subject to:

- Market demand  
- Order book depth  
- Counterparty availability  
- Exchange operational status  

There is no obligation on the issuer or administrator to provide continuous liquidity.

---

## 3. Market-Based Price Determination

The market price of NAX30 is determined solely through supply and demand dynamics on trading venues.

The token may trade at:

- A premium to theoretical index value  
- A discount to theoretical index value  
- Significant volatility relative to underlying benchmark movements  

There is no built-in arbitrage or redemption mechanism to align market price with indicative index value.

---

## 4. No Redemption Mechanism

NAX30 is non-redeemable.

Holders cannot:

- Redeem tokens for underlying [Namibia Securities Exchange (NSX)](https://nsx.com.na) listed securities  
- Exchange tokens for cash equivalent to net asset value  
- Demand buyback from the issuer  

All price realization occurs exclusively through voluntary market transactions.

---

## 5. Trading Risk

Secondary market participants assume full market risk, including but not limited to:

- Price volatility  
- Liquidity constraints  
- Spread widening  
- Slippage  
- Exchange suspension  
- Market disruption events  

Market participants may incur losses.

---

## 6. Exchange Listing Uncertainty

Any current or future exchange listing is subject to:

- Independent exchange approval  
- Compliance review  
- Operational readiness  
- Market conditions  

Listing on any exchange does not imply endorsement, regulatory approval, or investment suitability.

---

## 7. No Profit Expectation Representation

NAX30 is structured as a digital benchmark exposure instrument.

Nothing in its structure, documentation, or communications should be interpreted as:

- A promise of profit  
- A guaranteed return  
- A representation of capital appreciation  
- An income-generating instrument  

Market outcomes are uncertain and determined by market participants.

---

## 8. Market Integrity

The NAX30 governance framework includes:

- Multi-signature controls  
- Timelock execution delays  
- Transparent version control  
- Oracle quorum validation  

However, governance controls do not eliminate market risk.

---

## 9. Regulatory Variability

Secondary trading may be subject to:

- Jurisdictional securities interpretation  
- Exchange-specific compliance rules  
- Cross-border regulatory considerations  

Participants are responsible for ensuring compliance with applicable laws in their jurisdiction.

---

## 10. Structural Classification

NAX30 is:

- Fixed maximum supply  
- Governance-controlled mint  
- Non-redeemable  
- Market-priced  

Secondary market trading does not alter its structural classification.

---

## 11. Market Manipulation Risk

Secondary markets, particularly in early-stage or low-liquidity environments, may be susceptible to:

- Coordinated trading activity  
- Pump-and-dump schemes  
- Price distortions  
- Temporary volatility unrelated to benchmark fundamentals  

NAX30 governance does not control independent market participant behavior.

---

## 12. No Price Stabilization Obligation

Neither the issuer nor the administrator has any obligation to:

- Support or stabilize the market price  
- Provide price defense mechanisms  
- Intervene in open market trading  

Market prices may fluctuate without intervention.

---

## 13. Exchange & Counterparty Risk

Trading venues may:

- Suspend trading  
- Halt withdrawals  
- Freeze accounts  
- Experience operational outages  
- Become insolvent  

NAX30 governance does not control exchange counterparty risk.

Participants assume full counterparty exposure when using third-party platforms.

---

## 14. Network & Blockchain Risk

NAX30 is deployed on the BNB Smart Chain.

Risks include:

- Network congestion  
- Transaction delays  
- Gas fee volatility  
- Chain reorganization events  
- Smart contract vulnerabilities  
- Validator consensus risks  

Blockchain infrastructure risk is inherent to digital asset systems.

---

## 15. Tax Responsibility

Participants are solely responsible for:

- Determining tax implications  
- Reporting gains or losses  
- Complying with local tax regulations  

The issuer and administrator do not provide tax advice.

---

## 16. No Fiduciary Relationship

Ownership or trading of NAX30 does not create:

- A fiduciary relationship  
- An advisory relationship  
- An agency relationship  

The administrator does not act as investment adviser or fiduciary to token holders.

---

## 17. Market-Making Disclosure

There is no guarantee that any market-making services will be provided.

If market-making arrangements exist, they operate under separate commercial agreements and do not guarantee liquidity, price stability, or trading volume.

End of Secondary Market Disclosure.
