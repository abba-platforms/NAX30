## 1. Purpose

This Charter establishes the structure, authority, responsibilities, and operating procedures of the Risk Committee for the Namibia 30 Index (NAX30).

The Risk Committee is responsible for independent oversight of:

- Benchmark integrity risk  
- Governance risk  
- Smart contract risk  
- Oracle risk  
- Operational risk  
- Regulatory risk  
- Market structure risk  

The Committee operates as a supervisory body supporting institutional-grade benchmark administration.

## 2. Mandate

The Risk Committee shall:

- Identify and evaluate material risks affecting NAX30  
- Review smart contract upgrade risk  
- Review oracle configuration risk  
- Assess governance control risk  
- Monitor market structure and liquidity risk  
- Review regulatory developments impacting classification  
- Escalate material concerns to the Administrator and Index Committee  

The Committee does not directly execute governance transactions.

---

## 3. Authority

The Risk Committee may:

- Request documentation from the Administrator  
- Review governance proposals prior to execution  
- Recommend delay of governance actions  
- Issue formal risk memoranda  
- Recommend external audit engagement  

The Risk Committee may formally recommend postponement of governance execution if material risk is identified.

Final governance execution remains subject to Safe + Timelock structure.

The Risk Committee has no unilateral authority to alter smart contract parameters.

---

## 4. Composition

The Risk Committee shall consist of:

- At least three (3) members  
- Individuals with experience in finance, law, cybersecurity, blockchain systems, or risk management  

Members may include internal or independent external participants.

Members should demonstrate:

- Professional competence in risk evaluation  
- Independence from commercial token distribution decisions  
- Absence of material unresolved conflicts  

Appointments are approved through governance procedures.

---

## 5. Chairperson

The Risk Committee shall appoint a Chairperson.

The Chairperson shall:

- Set meeting agendas  
- Coordinate risk memoranda issuance  
- Escalate urgent findings  
- Ensure documentation integrity  
- Serve as primary liaison to Administrator  

Chair appointment is subject to Committee vote.

---

## 6. Quorum & Voting

Quorum requires a majority of appointed members.

Voting decisions require:

- Simple majority approval  
- In the event of tie, Chairperson holds casting vote  

Members must abstain where conflicts exist.

If quorum cannot be reached due to conflicts, independent advisory consultation may be recommended.

---

## 7. Independence

Risk Committee members must:

- Disclose conflicts of interest  
- Abstain from decisions where conflict exists  
- Act independently of commercial token distribution interests  

Benchmark integrity supersedes commercial objectives.

---

## 8. Risk Appetite Framework

NAX30 operates under a conservative risk posture prioritizing:

- Benchmark integrity  
- Governance transparency  
- Supply control discipline  
- Oracle robustness  
- Upgrade caution  

Material risk tolerance is low for:

- Governance override risk  
- Smart contract vulnerability  
- Oracle quorum compromise  

Higher tolerance may apply to market volatility, as token price is market-determined.

---

## 9. Risk Domains

The Committee shall monitor the following domains:

### 9.1 Smart Contract Risk
- Code vulnerabilities  
- Upgrade logic exposure  
- Access control integrity  
- Storage collision risk  

### 9.2 Governance Risk
- Multi-signature compromise risk  
- Timelock configuration risk  
- Parameter modification exposure  

### 9.3 Oracle Risk
- Quorum integrity (2-of-3)  
- Oracle collusion risk  
- Data manipulation risk  
- Liveness and outage scenarios  

### 9.4 Market Risk
- Liquidity concentration  
- Price divergence from theoretical index level  
- Exchange listing concentration risk  

### 9.5 Regulatory Risk
- Securities classification developments  
- MiCA or similar framework updates  
- Benchmark regulation applicability  

### 9.6 Operational Risk
- Documentation integrity  
- Governance execution errors  
- Key management failures  

---

## 10. Meeting Frequency

The Risk Committee shall meet:

- At least quarterly  
- Additionally upon material governance proposal  
- Upon detection of material security incident  

Meeting records shall be documented and retained for a minimum of five (5) years.

---

## 11. Reporting

The Risk Committee shall:

- Provide summary findings to the Administrator  
- Recommend remediation actions  
- Escalate urgent risks immediately  

Material risk findings may be disclosed publicly where appropriate and consistent with transparency policy.

---

## 12. Transparency Policy

The Committee may publish:

- Annual risk summary  
- Material risk event summary  
- Audit remediation status updates  

Confidential operational details may remain non-public where necessary for security.

---

## 13. Escalation Protocol

If a material risk is identified:

1. Risk memorandum issued  
2. Governance proposal may be paused via recommendation  
3. Administrator notified  
4. Emergency meeting convened if required  

Governance may delay execution via timelock if risk is substantiated.

---

## 14. Interaction with External Audit

The Risk Committee shall:

- Review external audit findings  
- Monitor remediation progress  
- Recommend re-audit if required  
- Evaluate severity classification consistency  

---

## 15. Removal & Replacement

Risk Committee members may be removed via governance for:

- Failure to attend meetings  
- Breach of confidentiality  
- Conflict of interest violation  
- Professional misconduct  

Members may resign voluntarily.

All changes must be documented.

---

## 16. Confidentiality

Risk Committee members may receive sensitive information.

Confidentiality must be maintained except where public disclosure is required for benchmark integrity.

---

## 17. Limitation of Authority

The Risk Committee:

- Does not execute smart contract upgrades  
- Does not hold custody of assets  
- Does not act as fiduciary  
- Does not guarantee elimination of risk  
- Does not override governance contracts  

The Committee provides oversight and recommendations only.

---

## 18. Annual Risk Assessment

The Risk Committee shall conduct an annual formal risk assessment covering:

- Smart contract risk  
- Governance controls  
- Oracle performance  
- Market structure stability  
- Regulatory developments  

A summary report may be published.

---

## 19. Charter Review

This Charter shall be reviewed annually.

Amendments require governance approval and documentation update.
