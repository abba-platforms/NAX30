## 1. Purpose

This Benchmark Statement of Compliance outlines the governance, methodology, operational, and transparency standards applicable to the Namibia 30 Index (NAX30).

This document is intended to formalize the structural integrity of NAX30 as a rules-based digital benchmark instrument.

This Statement is designed to align, where applicable, with recognized international benchmark governance principles, including high-level alignment with the IOSCO Principles for Financial Benchmarks. NAX30 is not currently designated as a regulated benchmark under any specific jurisdiction unless otherwise determined by applicable law.

## 3. Benchmark Nature

NAX30 is a blockchain-native benchmark index designed to reference a defined basket of thirty (30) securities listed on the Namibia Securities Exchange (NSX).

NAX30:

- Does not represent ownership in underlying securities  
- Does not constitute equity, debt, or fund units  
- Does not provide dividend entitlement  
- Does not incorporate redemption rights  

The index functions solely as a digital benchmark reference instrument.

---

## 4. Methodology Governance

The NAX30 Index operates under a published and version-controlled methodology document.

The methodology:

- Defines constituent eligibility criteria  
- Defines weighting logic  
- Defines calculation formula  
- Defines corporate action treatment  
- Defines rebalancing procedures  
- Defines market disruption policies  

All methodology changes require:

1. Multi-signature governance approval  
2. Timelock scheduling  
3. On-chain execution  
4. Public documentation update  

Material changes may be publicly disclosed prior to execution where operationally feasible.

---

## 5. Governance Framework

NAX30 governance is structured under a two-layer architecture:

- Multi-Signature Safe (proposal authority)  
- TimelockController (mandatory delay enforcement)  

Governance actions are:

- Publicly auditable  
- Event-emitting  
- Deterministically scheduled  
- Threshold-controlled  

No unilateral authority exists.

Governance does not exercise discretionary authority over calculated index levels.

---

## 6. Determination of Benchmark Level

The NAX30 index level is calculated algorithmically using a defined mathematical formula.

There is:

- No manual override of calculated values  
- No discretionary adjustment of published levels  
- No governance-based price setting  

All index calculations derive from predefined methodology inputs and quorum-confirmed oracle submissions.

---

## 7. Data Integrity & Oracle Controls

Index price inputs are obtained through a quorum-based oracle framework.

The system operates under a 2-of-3 quorum confirmation requirement.

Data validation controls include:

- Timestamp validation  
- Median-based aggregation logic  
- Tolerance thresholds  
- Public on-chain verification  

Oracle configuration changes require governance scheduling.

### Data Hierarchy

Primary Data Source:

- Official Namibia Securities Exchange (NSX) disclosures  

Secondary Sources:

- Validated market reporting systems  

In case of discrepancy, official exchange data prevails.

## 10. Conflict of Interest Principle

Governance participants:

- Must avoid conflicts of interest  
- Must not engage in price manipulation  
- Must not interfere with open market trading  
- Must not exercise discretion over index calculation  

NAX30 governance does not influence market pricing mechanisms.

## 12. Cessation Policy

NAX30 may be discontinued under extraordinary circumstances including:

- Regulatory prohibition  
- Prolonged exchange closure  
- Governance dissolution  
- Irrecoverable oracle failure  
- Legal constraints  

Cessation requires:

1. Multi-signature governance approval  
2. Timelock scheduling  
3. Public notification  

No retroactive adjustment of historical index values will occur upon cessation.
