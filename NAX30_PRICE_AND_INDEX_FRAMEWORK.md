# NAX30 Price and Index Framework  
## Institutional Reference Document  
*(For informational purposes only)*

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document establishes the structural, functional, and economic distinctions between the Namibia 30 Index (the “NAX30 Index”) and the NAX30 Token. It is intended to provide clarity to regulators, institutional counterparties, market participants, and technical integrators regarding the operation of the NAX30 ecosystem.

This document is not a prospectus, offering memorandum, or financial promotion. It does not constitute an invitation or solicitation to invest. It is provided solely for informational and reference purposes.

---

## 2. Definitions

For the purposes of this document:

The **“NAX30 Index”** refers to a rules-based benchmark designed to measure the performance of a defined group of securities listed on the Namibia Securities Exchange (NSX).

The **“NAX30 Token”** refers to a blockchain-based digital instrument issued with a fixed maximum supply and traded on supported digital asset exchanges.

The **“Index Value”** refers to the calculated level of the NAX30 Index derived from its methodology and underlying market data.

The **“Market Price”** refers to the price at which the NAX30 Token is traded on secondary markets.

The **“Participant”** refers to any individual or entity interacting with the NAX30 Token through market trading activity.

---

## 3. Structural Overview

The NAX30 ecosystem is composed of two distinct components: a benchmark reference layer and a market-traded digital instrument.

The NAX30 Index operates as a rules-based analytical construct designed to provide a transparent and methodology-driven reference for market performance. The NAX30 Token operates as a separate market instrument whose pricing is determined independently through trading activity.

These components are conceptually related but structurally independent.

## 4. The NAX30 Index

The NAX30 Index is a benchmark designed to reflect the performance of selected securities listed on the Namibia Securities Exchange (NSX). It is constructed and maintained in accordance with a defined methodology that governs constituent eligibility, weighting, rebalancing, and the treatment of corporate actions.

The Index serves as a reference measure for market performance and may be used for analytical, benchmarking, and potential product development purposes. It does not constitute an investment product, does not hold assets, and does not confer any ownership rights in the underlying securities.

The calculation of the Index is based on publicly available market data. While reasonable efforts are made to ensure consistency and methodological integrity, no representation is made as to the completeness or accuracy of such data at all times.

---

## 5. The NAX30 Token

The NAX30 Token is a digital instrument deployed on blockchain infrastructure with a fixed maximum supply of 10,000,000,000 units.

The Token is freely tradable on supported digital asset exchanges and functions as a market-based instrument. It is not redeemable for any underlying asset, is not backed by securities or financial reserves, and does not represent equity, debt, or any legal claim against any issuer.

Ownership of the NAX30 Token does not confer voting rights, dividend rights, or any entitlement to economic benefits derived from the underlying securities referenced by the Index.

---

## 6. Pricing Mechanism

The Market Price of the NAX30 Token is determined exclusively by supply and demand dynamics in the market.

There is no mechanism linking the Market Price of the Token to the Index Value. There is no redemption framework, no arbitrage structure, and no price stabilization mechanism designed to align the Token price with the Index.

Any initial listing price is a reference point only and does not represent intrinsic value, asset backing, or a valuation derived from the underlying securities.

Market participants should not expect any convergence between the Market Price of the Token and the Index Value, and such convergence is neither targeted nor supported by the structure of the system.

---

## 7. Separation of Index and Token

The NAX30 Index and the NAX30 Token operate independently in both structure and function.

The Index exists as an analytical benchmark calculated off-chain, while the Token exists as a market-traded digital instrument on blockchain infrastructure. The Index does not determine the price of the Token, and the Token does not influence the calculation of the Index.

The relationship between the two is conceptual rather than mechanical.

---

## 8. Absence of Asset Backing and Economic Rights

The NAX30 Token does not represent ownership of, or exposure to, the underlying securities referenced by the Index.

It does not entitle holders to dividends, distributions, voting rights, or any participation in the financial performance of those securities. It does not create any contractual or legal relationship between the Token holder and any listed issuer.

The Token is not structured as a collective investment scheme, exchange-traded fund, or pooled investment vehicle.

---

## 9. Data Sources and Index Integrity

The calculation of the NAX30 Index is based on publicly available market data, including exchange-provided pricing where applicable.

Data integrity is subject to the availability, accuracy, and timeliness of underlying data sources. Delays, revisions, or inaccuracies in source data may affect Index calculation.

The Index methodology is designed to provide consistency and transparency. Governance processes are expected to be in place to manage methodology updates, extraordinary events, and the treatment of exceptional market conditions.

---

## 10. Governance Framework

The NAX30 Index is intended to operate under a structured governance framework governing its methodology, maintenance, and oversight.

This framework is expected to include defined procedures for methodology changes, periodic review, and the management of conflicts of interest. Governance decisions are intended to follow a controlled and documented process rather than discretionary or ad hoc intervention.

The governance of the Index is distinct from the trading activity of the Token.

---

## 11. Institutional Interpretation

The NAX30 framework is designed to maintain a clear separation between benchmark definition and market trading.

The Index serves as a benchmark layer that may be referenced by institutional participants for analytical or product development purposes. The Token serves as a market-based instrument whose price reflects trading dynamics rather than underlying asset value.

Any regulated financial products referencing the Index, including exchange-traded funds or structured instruments, would be developed independently and subject to separate legal structuring and regulatory approval.

---

## 12. Risk Considerations

Participation in markets involving the NAX30 Token involves risks, including but not limited to price volatility, liquidity constraints, and market sentiment dynamics.

The absence of a linkage between Token price and Index Value means that pricing behavior may diverge significantly from the performance of the underlying benchmark.

Participants should not interpret the Token as a proxy for direct investment in the underlying securities.

---

## 13. Regulatory Positioning

The NAX30 Token is designed as a digital market instrument and is not intended to constitute a security, collective investment scheme, or regulated financial product representing pooled investment or asset ownership.

The NAX30 Index is a benchmark and not an investment vehicle.

Any future financial products referencing the Index will be subject to applicable regulatory requirements and approval processes.
