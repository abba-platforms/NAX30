## 4. U.S. Securities Law – Howey Analysis (Structural Considerations)

Under U.S. jurisprudence, an instrument may be classified as a security if it constitutes an “investment contract” under the Howey test.

The Howey framework examines:

1. Investment of money  
2. In a common enterprise  
3. With expectation of profit  
4. Derived primarily from the efforts of others  

Structural observations:

- NAX30 does not entitle holders to profit distributions.  
- There is no pooling of capital into a managed investment vehicle.  
- No asset custody is maintained for token holders.  
- Token value is determined solely by market supply and demand.  
- There is no contractual promise of managerial profit generation.  

This summary does not constitute a definitive legal determination.
